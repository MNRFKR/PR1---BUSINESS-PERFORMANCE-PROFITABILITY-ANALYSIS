Tactical Actions
- Bundling: Pair slow‑moving products with high‑demand items and sell them together at a discounted bundle price. This helps clear excess stock while boosting perceived value.
- Discounts & Promotions: Offer targeted markdowns, flash sales, or loyalty program rewards to accelerate turnover.
- Channel Diversification: Move excess stock through alternative channels (e.g., online marketplaces, outlet stores, B2B bulk sales).
- Seasonal Tie‑ins: Reposition items with seasonal campaigns or rebranding to align with current demand cycles.
- Cross‑selling: Train sales teams to recommend slow‑moving items alongside popular products.

Strategic Inventory Management
- Demand Forecasting: Use historical sales data and external factors (seasonality, promotions, competitor pricing) to better predict demand and avoid future overstock.
- Safety Stock Optimization: Adjust safety stock levels to balance between avoiding stockouts and preventing excess.
- KPIs & Monitoring: Track metrics like inventory turnover ratio, days sales of inventory (DSI), and gross margin return on investment (GMROI) to identify problem items early.
- Supplier Collaboration: Negotiate flexible order quantities or return agreements with suppliers to reduce risk of overstock.
- Lean Practices: Adopt just‑in‑time (JIT) or demand‑driven replenishment to minimize excess inventory.

Risks & Trade‑offs
- Heavy discounting can erode brand value if overused.
- Bundling may reduce margins but is effective for clearing stock.
- Over‑reliance on promotions can train customers to wait for discounts.
- Cutting safety stock too aggressively risks stockouts and lost sales.
