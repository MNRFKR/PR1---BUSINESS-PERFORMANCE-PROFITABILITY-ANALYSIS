📘 Introduction

This project focuses on analyzing retail sales data to derive insights into slow‑moving and overstocked items.

The dataset includes attributes such as Region, Category, Seasonality, Inventory Level, Units Sold, Units Ordered, Price, Discount, Demand, and Promotion. The goal is to build a Power BI dashboard that highlights sales performance, profit margins, and inventory dynamics, enabling strategic decision‑making.



📄 Abstract

The analysis identifies categories and regions where inventory levels are disproportionately high compared to demand and sales. 

By calculating Total Sales, Profit, Profit Margin, Units Sold, and Inventory vs Demand, the dashboard highlights inefficiencies in stock management.

This empowers businesses to take corrective actions such as targeted promotions, bundling, or supplier renegotiations.



🛠 Tools Used

\- Excel/CSV dataset (raw sales data sourced from the internet for demonstration purposes)

\- SQL: Querying and structuring raw sales data for analysis.

\- Python: Data cleaning, transformation, and statistical checks.

\- Power BI Desktop for data modeling and visualization

\- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) for creating measures like Total Sales, Profit, and Profit Margin



🔄 Steps Involved in Building the Project

\- Data Import: Load the raw dataset (sales\_data) into Power BI.

\- Data Cleaning: Ensure column names are consistent (e.g., Units Sold, Price, Discount).

\- Measure Creation: Define DAX measures for Total Sales, Profit, Profit Margin, Units Sold, and Inventory Turnover.

\- Visualization:

\- Cards for KPIs (Sales, Profit, Margin)

\- Bar/Column charts for Sales by Region/Category

\- Line charts for Seasonality trends

\- Scatter plots for Inventory vs Demand

\- Slicers: Add filters for Region, Category, and Seasonality to make the dashboard interactive.

\- Analysis: Identify slow‑moving and overstocked items by comparing Inventory Level vs Demand and Units Ordered vs Units Sold.



📊 $$$ - **Strategic Suggestions**

\- # For Slow‑Moving Items

* \- Introduce targeted discounts or loyalty rewards.
* \- Bundle with fast‑moving products to increase visibility.
* \- Reposition with seasonal promotions or marketing campaigns.

\- # For Overstocked Items

* \- Launch clearance sales or flash promotions.
* \- Explore alternative channels (online marketplaces, B2B bulk deals).
* \- Negotiate flexible supply terms with vendors to reduce future overstock.

\- # Preventive Measures

* \- Use demand forecasting to align procurement with seasonality.
* \- Monitor KPIs like Inventory Turnover Ratio and Days Sales of Inventory (DSI).
* \- Apply lean practices (just‑in‑time replenishment) to minimize excess stock.



✅ Conclusion

This Power BI project demonstrates how a raw sales dataset can be transformed into actionable insights.

By highlighting slow‑moving and overstocked items, businesses can optimize inventory, reduce carrying costs, and improve profitability.

The dashboard serves as a practical decision‑support tool, bridging raw data with strategic outcomes in a clear, interactive format.
